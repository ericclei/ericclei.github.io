Introduction

I am a PhD student in the Machine Learning Department at CMU. I am interested in applied machine learning using data mining, unsupervised learning, interpretable models, and Bayesian methods. I design novel techniques to apply to domains such as nuclear physics and medicine. My advisor is Artur Dubrawski. My expected date of graduation is Spring 2019. I started my PhD in 2014 when I was 20 years old.

Current Projects

Internal Bleeding Prediction Using Clinical Phenotypes

We explore the capability of central venous pressure to predict internal bleeding. Subjects are partitioned into phenotypes to enhance predictions and infer relevant clinical information. To obtain these partitions we employ various traditional and novel clustering algorithms. This project is joint work with Dr. Michael Pinsky's team from the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center.

Simultaneous Estimation of Background Noise and Source Strength

In radiation, source detection can be accomplished by several methods that assume the existence of training data for learning characteristics of background radiation. In practice, training data may be scarce or unreliable. We develop a source detection method that does not rely on training data by dynamically estimating the background spectrum simultaneously with source strength. The method may be categorized as an adaptive Kalman filter. This project is joint work with Simon Labov and Karl Nelson from the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. [working paper] [slides]
